Overview

This is an experiment in decision-making. Purdue University has provided funds for this research. The instructions are simple. If you follow them carefully and make good decisions, you can earn an appreciable amount of money. These earnings will be paid to you privately in cash at the end of the experiment.

We ask that you not talk with one another for the duration of the experiment. Please turn off your cell-phones. Do not use e-mail.

During the course of this experiment, you will be called upon to make decisions in several periods. The experiment is divided into five sequences of periods and each sequence is referred to as a cycle.

  • At the beginning of a cycle, each participant in this room will be randomly assigned to a set.
  • In each set there will be seven persons.
  • One of the participants in each set will be randomly chosen to be the observer throughout the whole cycle. The other six participants are active participants.
  • If you are an active participant, for the whole duration of a cycle, you will interact exclusively with the five other active participants in your set and nobody else. Every active participant in a set will be given a fixed ID: 1, 2, 3, 4, 5 or 6.
  • If you are the observer , for the whole duration of a cycle, you will be observing what the other six active participants are doing.
  • You might meet again these participants in the following cycles, but you won’t be able to recognize each other because ID numbers are randomly assigned at the start of each cycle.
  • Your role as the observer or active participant in one cycle is not dependent on your role in a previous cycle.

    • Interaction rules (1/3)

    The following rules only apply to active participants!

    In each period of a cycle:

  • In each period you will be matched to one other active participant selected at random from the set you are assigned to. We will refer to this person as “your match”.
  • You will not be informed of the identity of your match. Hence, you do not know whether you have already interacted with that person in previous periods of the same cycle.
  • You and your match will interact according to the rules that will be explained in a moment.
  • After each period you will be re-matched to an active participant chosen at random from the set you are assigned to. There is one chance out of five that you will be matched with any given active participants in your set.

    In a period, you and your match can make either of two choices, Y or Z. The points you earn in a period depend upon both the choice you make, and the choice made by your match in that period. As the payoff table above indicates, there are four possible outcomes:

    1. If both of you choose Y this period then: you both earn 25 points.
    2. If you choose Ythis period and your match chooses Z then: you earn 5 points, and your match earns 30 points.
    3. If you choose Z this period and your match chooses Y then: you earn 30 points, and your match earns 5 points.
    4. If you both choose Z then: you both earn 10 points.

    To make your choice in each period, click the option button next to either Y or Z. You may change your mind at any time prior to clicking the “Submit” button by simply clicking on the option button next to Y or Z. You are free to choose Y or Z in every period. When you are satisfied with your choice, click the “Submit” button. After all persons have made their choices, the results of the period will appear on your screen.

    Interaction rules (2/3)

    The following rules only apply to active participants!

    The result screen will display the number of points you have earned for the period along with your choice and the choice of your match . The first column of the ‘Summary of Results’ table contains your past choices. The second column concerns the choices of your previous matches. Notice that choices in the second column were most likely made by different persons in different periods. You are not given information on the choices made by the other four persons in your set.

    At this stage a ball will be drawn from a “virtual” urn containing one hundred balls numbered from 1 to 100. Each ball is equally likely to be selected. The computer program will randomly draw a ball and show the number on the result screen. If this random number is less than or equal to {{continuation_chance}}, then the cycle will continue into the next period. If this number is greater than {{continuation_chance}}, then the cycle ends. Therefore, after each period there is a {{continuation_chance}}% chance that there will be another period of interaction in the cycle and a {{end_chance}}% chance that the cycle will end.

    Suppose that a number less than or equal to 90 has been drawn. Then you press the “Next” button to proceed. You will face the same decisional situation as in the previous period, but with a person selected at random from the set of active participants you were assigned to. Remember that there are six active participants in each set.

    Before making your choice, you may review all the outcomes in previous periods of the cycle from the “Summary of Results” table. The table shows your history and the past choices of the persons who happened to be your match in the period. You then choose either Y or Z. Your choice and the choice of your match this period are recorded and added to the “Summary of Results” table in the lower portion of your screen.

    If the number drawn is greater than {{continuation_chance}} then the cycle ends. When a cycle ends, you will be notified in a new screen. There will be a total of five cycles. The rules in the following cycles are the same as in the first, but you will interact with different people. More precisely, after each cycle, new sets of individuals will be formed. This random assignment does not depend on any previous choices. A participant might interact with a person for more than one cycle. In each future cycle, your chance of being selected to be the observer is 1/7, whereas your chance of being selected to be an active participant is 6/7.

    Interaction rules (3/3)

    The following rules only apply to the observer!

    In each period of a cycle:

  • You can observe the whole history of choices of all six active participants in your set.
  • You will not interact with active participants.
  • You get {{Constants.observer_payoff}}

    Earnings

    The points you earned in each period are summed up at the end of the experiment. For every 100 points that you earn you will receive 1 dollar ($1.00). Therefore, the more points you earn the more money you earn. In addition, you will receive a $5 show-up fee. You will be paid your earnings in cash and in private at the end of today’s session.


    Final Comments

    First, do not discuss your choices or your results with anyone at any time during the experiment.

    Second, your ID# is private. Do not reveal it to anyone.

    Third, since there is a {{continuation_chance}}% chance that at the end of a period the cycle will continue. However, since the stopping decision is made randomly, some cycles may be much longer than 20 periods and some others may be much shorter.

    Fourth, if you are an active participant , remember that after each period you will be matched randomly to someone in the set you were assigned to. As there are six active participants in the set, the probability of you being matched with the same person in two consecutive periods of a cycle is 1/5. You are not told the identity of your match.

    Fifth, if you are the observer, your role is fixed within a cycle.

    Sixth, the rules are the same in all five cycles. After a cycle, your chance of being an active participant is 6/7 while your chance of being the observer is 1/7.
